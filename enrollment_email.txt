After reviewing the project proposals presented on March 27th, I would

combining two of the advertised sub-projects into a single coherent
work plan:

  1. "Donut Ranges in VRA" - extending the Value Range Analysis pass
     to represent unions of intervals, so that value distributions
     clustered around a non-zero mean (e.g. neural-network weights)
     are not forced to include the near-zero region that causes range
     explosion in division and reciprocal operations.

  2. "Range Models for NN Activation Functions" - teaching VRA the
     input/output behaviour of the standard activation functions
     (sigmoid, tanh, ReLU and its variants, ELU, softplus, GELU, SiLU)
     and, where appropriate, providing fixed-point implementations
     based on lookup tables or polynomial approximations.

My motivation for combining the two sub-projects is that they are
mathematically complementary: a sigmoid applied to a donut-shaped
input such as [-10, -5] U [5, 10] produces a donut-shaped output
(approximately [0, 0.007] U [0.993, 1.0]) that cannot be captured by
a single interval without collapsing to the trivial [0, 1]. Donut
ranges are therefore the natural representation that lets activation
function modelling deliver its full benefit, and together they form
a single coherent contribution to TAFFO's handling of neural networks.

### Proposed work plan

  * Phase 1 - Finish and harden the activation function range handlers
    in TaffoVRA/RangeOperationsCallWhitelist.cpp, including closed-form
    treatment of non-monotonic functions (GELU, SiLU) and LLVM intrinsic
    entries where applicable.

  * Phase 2 - Design and implement a DonutRange type in TaffoVRA: a
    canonicalised union of disjoint closed intervals, with union /
    intersection / widening and the four arithmetic operations. The
    new type is introduced in a way that keeps the existing Range
    API working (convex-hull fallback for downstream passes), and is
    enabled via an opt-in command-line flag so existing TAFFO users
    are not affected.

  * Phase 3 - Plumb DonutRange through VRAnalyzer, VRAGlobalStore and
    VRAFunctionStore, and extend the activation function handlers to
    propagate donut ranges piecewise.

  * Phase 4 - Unit tests covering canonicalisation, arithmetic,
    serialisation and activation-function interaction.

  * Phase 5 - Benchmarking on at least one small neural network
    (for example a quantised MLP on MNIST) comparing baseline TAFFO,
    TAFFO with activation models only, and TAFFO with donut ranges +
    activation models. I will report per-variable bit-widths assigned
    by DTA, end-to-end accuracy, and binary size.

  * Phase 6 - A written report covering motivation, formalism,
    implementation, evaluation and directions for a possible thesis
    extension.
